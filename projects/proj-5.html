Spring 2012

At the end of my sophomore year at Tufts, I worked with 3 other classmates on a digital logic final project: designing and building a videogame on an 8x8 LED array using LSI/MSI/SSI logic. After investing about 100 total man-hours, we successfully interfaced on a breadboard the circuitry for a clock, row generator, user controls, memory, score keeper, game display, collision detector, and character row generator.
